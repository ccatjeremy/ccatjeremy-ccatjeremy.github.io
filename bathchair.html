Bath Chair
Industry-University Cooperative Project , 2015
Chen YuWen
Hu JingTing
About bath chair
My industrial design class in junior year was cooperated with Taitung St’ MARY’s Hospital, the caregivers told us what they need, and we helped them improve the bath chair. It’s a good opportunity for us to know the problems and needs of caregivers.
What do the Caregivers need?
We interviewed caregivers from Tainan and Taitung, and the chief of wheelchair manufacturer to discuss the real needs of caregivers. Their experience helped us a lot.

Foot brake is hard to use
The caregivers have to step the brakes for 4 times for setting the bath chair, and more problems they will faced when helping the elder take a bath in the narrow bathroom.

Slide down easily:
Because of the elder’s weak calf, they will easily slide down when took bath.
Dangerous slit
For some manufacturing reasons, lots of foot boards are not made in a complete plane, and it might make elder’s toes stuck in the slit and get hurt easily.

Only one side to sit down
It’s a terrible problem for caregivers when they move the elders. More flexible armrests will give them more choices.
Hand brake:
Two-stages brake levers are more suitable for the condition caregivers using. They could pull the brake while they are moving the elder, and press down the brake while helping the elder take a bath.

Tilted seat:
We designed the tilted seat to change the center of gravity of the body closing to the chair back, and avoided the elder sliding down while taking a bath.
Support cloth:
When the caregivers bathe the elder’s hips, they don’t need to hold the elder from the front side.

Armrest can be lifted up:
Moving the elder is easier. There are two more directions to transfer the elders to the bed.
Complete foot board:
It will be safe and the elder’s toes will not stuck in the slit anymore.
